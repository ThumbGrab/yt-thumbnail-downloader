How to Write YouTube Descriptions That Rank

Your YouTube description is a powerful but often overlooked SEO tool. A well-written description helps YouTube understand your content, improves your search ranking, and gives viewers important information and links. You have up to 5,000 characters to work with.

The Most Important Part

The first 2-3 lines of your description appear above the fold before viewers click "show more." This is prime real estate. Put your most important keyword and a compelling summary here to hook viewers and signal relevance to YouTube's algorithm.

Description Best Practices

  • Include your main keyword in the first sentence naturally
  • Write at least 200 words for better SEO
  • Add timestamps to improve viewer experience and watch time
  • Include links to related videos, playlists, and social media
  • Add a clear call to action to like and subscribe
  • End with 3-5 relevant hashtags

Why Timestamps Matter

Adding timestamps (also called chapters) to your description breaks your video into sections. This improves the viewer experience, can increase watch time, and sometimes earns you key moments in Google search results — giving your video extra visibility.
